What are tokens? #

The input text given to a LLM is broken down into tokens which have numerical representation that can easily be understood by LLM. Tokens are basically a collection of chars. The length of a token can range from a single char to couple of chars. for example the below sentence has 53 tokens and 252 characters

Many words map to one token, but some don't: indivisible.

Unicode characters like emojis may be split into many tokens containing the underlying bytes: ü§öüèæ

Sequences of characters commonly found next to each other may be grouped together: 1234567890

you can try generating tokens form text using this site

A helpful rule of thumb is that one token corresponds to 4 chars of english text.

Tokenization #

The process of converting text to tokens is called tokenization.
